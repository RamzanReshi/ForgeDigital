# FORGE DIGITAL — THE PLAN (final, lock-in version)

*Built on the $100M Offers / $100M Leads framework. One page. No changes for 30 days.*

## 1. Why "a website" was the wrong offer

Hormozi's value equation: **Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood it works) ÷ (Time delay × Effort required)**.

"A website" scores badly — it's a commodity, the owner can't connect it to money, and Fiverr sells one for $300. Nobody's dream outcome is owning a website. Their dream outcome is **more customers calling and booking**. So we sell that, and the website is just the delivery mechanism.

## 2. The Grand Slam Offer

**"The New Client Engine" — $2,000 flat, live in 5 business days.**

What's in the box (each piece named and priced so the stack feels like $5k+):

| Piece | What the client hears |
|---|---|
| Multilingual website (EN + their community language) | "Customers who search in Arabic/Turkish/Spanish find YOU, not your competitor" ($2,500 value) |
| Google Business Profile optimization | "Show up on the map when locals search" ($500 value) |

| 30 days of free edits after launch | "We don't disappear after delivery" ($300 value) |

**Guarantee (kills the risk):** "You approve the design before you pay the balance. If you don't love the homepage we show you, you owe nothing beyond the deposit — and we'll refund that too if we miss the 5-day deadline." We can afford this because our build cost is near zero.

**Scarcity (real, not fake):** "We take 4 clients a month. Founding price $2,000 — goes to $3,500 after the first four."

**Payment:** $1,000 deposit via Payoneer to start, $1,000 on approval. Optional $97/mo "Engine Maintenance" after month one (edits, updates, review monitoring) — this retainer is what actually builds toward the million.

## 3. Who we sell to (pick ONE niche for 30 days)

Diaspora-owned service businesses in the US & UK whose customers search in two languages and whose websites are bad or missing. Starting niche: **restaurants, barbershops, and clinics owned by Turkish/Arab/South-Asian owners in London, NYC/New Jersey, and Toronto.** Narrow niche = every email, call, and mockup gets sharper and referrals travel inside the community.

## 4. Leads — the Core Four, our version

$100M Leads says there are only four ways: warm outreach, cold outreach, content, paid ads. With no budget and no audience, we run the first two hard:

1. **Warm outreach (Ramzan + Ahmed, day 1):** 30+ personal contacts each get the founding-client message. Fastest possible first deal.
2. **Cold outreach with a "lead magnet in the first message":** Hormozi's rule — give value before asking. Ours is the personalized homepage mockup. Claude generates it; the prospect sees their own business looking better. Email the screenshot (Ramzan), call the next day (Ahmed): "Did you see the new homepage we built for you?"
3. Content and paid ads: not yet. After 5 closed clients, we post before/after case studies (content), and only after $10k revenue do we test ads.

**Volume commitment (Hormozi's "rule of 100"):** 100 outreach actions per day combined — 20 new leads sourced, 20 mockup emails, 30 calls, 30 follow-ups. Every single day. The math: at even 1% close on mockup-warmed leads, 100/day = first client inside 2 weeks, then compounding referrals.
