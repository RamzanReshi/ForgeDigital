## The sequence (per lead)

1. **Day 0:** email with mockup screenshot attached.
2. **Day 1:** call. Reference the mockup — never a blind pitch.
3. **Day 3:** follow-up email ("bumping this — the mockup link expires Friday").
4. **Day 5:** second call. Then move on. Six touches max, no chasing after that.

>
> We're Forge Digital. We build modern sites in English + [Arabic/Turkish/Spanish] for businesses like yours, delivered in 5 days, flat $2,000. If you like the direction, I'll send a live preview link.
>
> Worth a 10-minute call? I'm at [US number].
>
> Ramzan — Forge Digital

Rules: plain text, no HTML templates, one attachment (the mockup), send 20–30/day max per mailbox while the domain is new, always from the .co address.

## Call script (Day 1)

**Opener:** "Hi, is this [name]? This is Ramzan from Forge Digital — I emailed you yesterday, the new homepage design for [business]. Did you get a chance to see it?"

- **"Yes, saw it":** "What did you think?" → shut up and listen. Whatever they say, pivot: "The full site is $2,000 flat, live in 5 days, in English and [language]. Want me to send the live preview so you can click around?"
- **"No / what email?":** "No problem — I'll resend it while we talk. Thirty seconds of context: we redesign websites for [type] businesses, and yours came up because [specific reason: no mobile site / outdated / no Arabic version]. Can I get your best email?"
- **"Not interested":** "Totally fine. One question before I go — is that because the current site is doing its job, or just bad timing?" (This one line revives ~1 in 5 rejections.) If still no: "Understood, have a good one." Log it, next call.
- **"How much?":** Give the number immediately. "$2,000 flat, everything included, 5-day delivery, then $50 a month only if you want us maintaining it. No surprises." Hiding price kills trust.

**Close:** never end without a next step — a booked call time, a preview link promise, or a deposit. "I'll send the Payoneer link for the $1,000 deposit and we start today" is the sentence that makes money.
